## EARLY ANIMATION PIONEERS (pre-1930s)

1. Koko the Clown — the original rubber-hose trickster who leaps from Max Fleischer's inkwell into the real world; first animated star built with the rotoscope (*Out of the Inkwell*, 1918, Fleischer Studios)
2. Felix the Cat — the world's first great cartoon superstar; sly, black-and-white, and utterly surreal with his magic bag of tricks (*Felix the Cat*, 1919, Pat Sullivan / Otto Messmer)
3. Gertie the Dinosaur — the gentle brachiosaurus who took a bow and ate a mammoth on command; the first animated character with a distinct personality (*Gertie the Dinosaur*, 1914, Winsor McCay)
4. Oswald the Lucky Rabbit — Walt Disney and Ub Iwerks's elastic, floppy-eared bunny precursor to Mickey, lost to Universal and rediscovered a century later (*Oswald the Lucky Rabbit*, 1927)
5. Steamboat Willie Mickey Mouse — the whistling, mischief-making mouse who launched synchronized-sound animation and built an empire (*Steamboat Willie*, 1928, Walt Disney / Ub Iwerks)
6. Bimbo — Fleischer's good-natured round-nosed black dog, Koko's partner-in-crime and Betty Boop's early beau (*Fleischer Studios*, 1930)
7. Bosko — the rubber-hose kid who was Looney Tunes' very first star, debuting in a sound-synchronized pilot before Mickey's first talkie (*Looney Tunes*, 1929–1930, Harman-Ising)
8. Flip the Frog — Ub Iwerks's first post-Disney creation: a stumpy, wide-eyed frog who starred in the first color sound cartoon (*Flip the Frog*, 1930, Ub Iwerks)
9. Betty Boop — the jazz-baby flapper with the baby voice and the garter; queen of Fleischer Studios and early animation's most iconic leading lady (*Betty Boop*, 1930, Fleischer Studios)
10. Popeye the Sailor — the spinach-fueled, one-eyed mariner who punches his way through anything; started in comics and became a cartoon titan (*Thimble Theatre / Fleischer*, 1929/1933)
11. Olive Oyl — Popeye's gangly, shrieking, perpetually-kidnapped sweetheart; a comedic chaos magnet of staggering proportions (*Thimble Theatre*, 1919)
12. Bluto (Brutus) — Popeye's boulder-fisted nemesis; the ur-cartoon bully who never once wins (*Thimble Theatre*, 1932)
13. Wimpy — the gentlemanly mooch who will gladly pay you Tuesday for a hamburger today; deadpan comic genius (*Thimble Theatre*, 1931)
14. Ko-Ko's Inkwell Creatures — the amorphous ink-blobs and shape-shifting dream-horrors that haunt the Fleischer inkwell universe; pure surrealist nightmare fuel (*Out of the Inkwell*, 1919–1929)
15. The Cab Calloway Ghost Walrus — the rubber-boneless dancing specter in the Betty Boop short "Minnie the Moocher," rotoscoped from Cab Calloway's live performance; animation's eeriest cameo (*Betty Boop*, 1932, Fleischer)
